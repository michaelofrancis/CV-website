Summary

Hello! I am a Ph. D. Candidate in the Institute of Bioinformatics at the University of Georgia. My projected Ph. D. graduation date is December 17th, 2022.

Our lab's research is focused on discovering novel genetic variants that affect lipid metabolism in humans. These variants are identified using state-of-the-art computational methods applied to biobank-scale datasets. Specifically we perform GWAS (genome-wide association studies) and GWIS (genome-wide interaction studies) on the genotype and phenotype data collected in the UK Biobank. We also replicate our significant findings in external cohorts. Our goals are to uncover new biological connections between dietary intake and individual genetics, and to contibute to the development of gene-based dietary recommendations, AKA "precision nutrition."

The primary coding languages I use to perform this research are R and Unix shell. I have moderate experience using Python and Java. I am also experienced in web development using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. The code for I wrote for this website can be found HERE!

I am looking for career opportunities to apply the knowledge I have gained in my Ph. D. research in the following domains: genetic epidemiology, nutrition science, computer programming, statistics, and scientific writing.
